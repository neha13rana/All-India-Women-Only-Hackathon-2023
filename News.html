2. Powering the future sustainable mobility in india's business ecosystem

Charging infrastructure: In 2016, India had less than 500 EV charging stations across Delhi, Mumbai,
Bengaluru, and Kolkata, which increased to 993 by the end of July 2020. It is estimated that by 2030,
Delhi will need around 300,000 high-speed charging stations.

EV30@30 Campaign: The initiative aims for EV sales share to reach 30 percent by 2030. The Campaign
sees potential and supports the market for electric passenger cars, light commercial vans, buses, and trucks
and focuses on the charging infrastructure needed to supply sufficient power to the vehicles deployed.

3. Powering sustainable mobility with technology

In response to the surge in transportation demand and its impact on the environment,
the Indian government is accelerating the 'Smart Mobility' program, integrating vehicle
intelligence, autonomous systems, and communications. Shared mobility, electric vehicles,
and Mobility as a Service (MaaS) are transforming urban mobility. Technology
integration enhances communication, real-time updates, and driving experience,
promoting eco-friendly transportation. Sustainable mobility plays a pivotal role in tackling climate
change and advancing environmental sustainability, offering convenient solutions for individuals
and benefiting employee well-being. The transportation sector's transformation towards greener options
is essential for a sustainable future.
